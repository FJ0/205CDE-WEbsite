Number 1 - Lirik

Lirik is my favourite streamer, he streams everyday 5pm-11pm and later if it is a night stream(with Waffle and shortyyguy). Sunday is "Sub-Sunday" where subscribers can vote on the game for him to play next. Lirik plays almost every newly released game and is watched by over 45,000 people daily. I like Lirik because he is comical whilst still being able to compete at the highest level of play and is highly skilled in almost every game he plays. Chat interaction is Lirik's main reason why I watch Lirik almost everyday, even though there are more than 45,000 people in his chat, he responds and answers questions and comments throughout the stream.

Number 2 - Summit

Jaryd Lazar "Summit1G" (Half Hungarian) originally started streaming Counter Strike in the early days. Once WarZ (aka 'Infestation: Survivor Stories') got released, he got playing the game at the right time and his channel grew into the channel where you see him now but he also grew as a streamer. He became a competitive CS: GO player on ESEA (playing on Invite teams) and FPL ( playing against the US CS: GO Pros). Before streaming professionally or taking streaming seriously as a Full Time job, Jaryd worked as a call representative for Time Warner Cable. Jaryd then married his longtime girlfriend, Desirae, on April 24, 2013.

Watch live video from summit1g on www.twitch.tv

Number 3 - AmazHS

Jason "AmazHS" was the first member of Team Liquid's Hearthstone division. In the Blizzard 2014 Stream Awards, Jason won the "Best Hearthstone Streamer in 2014" award in the poll categories. He came in second place for "Most Active Chat" and fifth place for "Highest Mean Viewership" for overall Blizzard games on Twitch.tv. In November 2014, Jason formed Team Archon after leaving Team Liquid. He immediately signed the Blizzcon 2014 November Hearthstone world champion James "Firebat" Kostesich.

Number 4 - BobRoss

The Bob Ross twitch stream is a highlight of my week, one of the best streams on Twitch. Every Monday night, the stream shows reruns of the classic The Joy of Painting which on average is viewed by 45,000 people a week. As part of the launch of Twitch Creative, Twitch.tv hosted a nine-day marathon of Bob Ross' The Joy of Painting series which started on October 29, 2015 in commemoration of what would have been his 73rd birthday. Twitch reported that 5.6 million viewers watched the marathon, and due to its popularity, created a weekly rebroadcast with one season of The Joy of Painting to air on Twitch each Monday, and will have a marathon of episodes each October 29th. A portion of the advertising revenue has been promised to charities, including St. Jude Children's Research Hospital.

Watch live video from BobRoss on www.twitch.tv

Number 5 - GamesDoneQuick

Let me start off by saying, if GamesDoneQuick streamed everyday - I would watch them over any other channel. This being said, GDQ only have two events a year which last around 2 weeks each. Awesome Games Done Quick and Summer Games Done Quick are two annual video game speedrun charity marathons held in the United States, managed by the communities at Speed Demos Archive and Speedruns Live. Held since 2010, the events have raised money for several charities the most common two being the Prevent Cancer Foundation and Doctors Without Borders. The events are broadcast live on Twitch. Viewers are encouraged to donate for incentives during the stream such as naming characters in a run, having the runners attempt more difficult challenges, or winning prizes. Over $6.7 million has been raised across thirteen marathons.

Watch live video from GamesDoneQuick on www.twitch.tv
